Core Interests

2D Materials

Synthesis and characterization of graphene, MXene (Ti₃C₂Tₓ), and transition metal dichalcogenides (MoS₂, WS₂, WSe₂, MoSe₂). Focus on large-scale growth and structural properties.

My proposed research investigates the optoelectronic properties of 2D materials grown via Chemical Vapor Deposition. Unlike mechanical exfoliation, CVD enables large-area, single-crystal TMD films on wafer-scale substrates — bridging the gap between laboratory-scale physics and industrial photonic technology.

  • Salt-assisted CVD for centimeter-scale single-crystal WS₂ domains (>100 μm)
  • MOCVD reactor scaling for 4-inch wafer uniformity (<5% thickness variation)
  • Prototype broadband photodetectors targeting R > 10³ A/W
  • van der Waals heterostructure LEDs with electroluminescence at 600–800 nm
  • Raman, PL, HRTEM, AFM, XPS, Hall effect, and TRPL characterization suite
  • ML-guided real-time CVD optimization via optical emission spectroscopy

Quantum Computing · VQE · 2025–2026

Quantum VQE Simulation for 2D Material Band Gap Prediction

Applied Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) to estimate band gaps of Ti₃C₂ MXene (0.00 eV) and WS₂ monolayer (1.98 eV) using 2-qubit Pauli Hamiltonians and COBYLA optimization. Achieved 100% accuracy versus DFT literature.
